About the Legend
Who is MSD?

Mahendra Singh Dhoni was born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand. From a small railway town to becoming the most celebrated captain in Indian cricket history — his journey is one of the most remarkable sporting stories ever told.

Known as Captain Cool, Dhoni's ability to remain calm under pressure became his defining trait. Whether it was the 2011 World Cup final six, a last-ball finish, or a crisis situation — Dhoni thrived where others froze.

His lightning-quick wicketkeeping, unorthodox batting, and ice-cold decision-making as captain made him irreplaceable. He is the only captain in history to win all three ICC trophies — T20 World Cup, ODI World Cup, and Champions Trophy.

The Journey
Career Timeline
2004
International Debut
Made his ODI debut against Bangladesh. Scored 0 — but India saw something special in the young keeper from Ranchi.
2005
183* vs Sri Lanka — The Arrival
Smashed 183* off 145 balls in Jaipur — the highest ODI score by a wicketkeeper. The world sat up and noticed.
2007
T20 World Cup — First Captain, First Title
Led India to the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup victory in South Africa. Famous for giving the final over to Joginder Sharma — a masterstroke that stunned Pakistan.
2008
Full-Time Captain + IPL Begins
Appointed Test captain. Also became the first captain of Chennai Super Kings in the IPL — beginning a dynasty that would win 5 IPL titles.
2011
ODI World Cup — That Six
Led India to their second ODI World Cup on home soil. Promoted himself up the batting order in the final and finished it with a towering six over long-on — one of cricket's most iconic moments.
2013
Champions Trophy — The Treble
Won the ICC Champions Trophy, becoming the only captain in history to win all three ICC limited-overs trophies.
2020
Retirement — A Quiet Goodbye
Announced his international retirement on Instagram on Independence Day — no press conference, no fanfare. Just a short video. Perfectly Dhoni.
